As for my scientific interests, they are mostly based on labarotory-base research combined with theory to explain the interactions between two neighbouring surfaces in fluids that can define whether those surfaces stick together or will be separated. That might explain many natural phenomena such as earthquakes, rock-fracturing, landslides and avalanches, and sinking cities like Venice in Italy (that is sinking into its surrounding lagoon over the centuries). Studying the interaction forces can also help the pharmaceutical industry to understand the interaction between drug paricles and human cells; in the paint industry the interaction between particles of the coloidal dispersion (paint) and the wall decides whether the paint adhere to the wall, under any circumastances, or comes off. To summarize, the interaction forces exist between all elements around us and become effective depending on the distance between the interacting elements. In a wiser word: "These forces govern the behaviour of everyday things."
